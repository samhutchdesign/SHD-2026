# FAQ / Objection Handling

> The concerns clients actually have before hiring a freelance designer, each with Sam's answer. Feeds a site "FAQ / How I work" section and informs quiz result copy. Answers are drafts in Sam's voice — confirm specifics (price, availability, IP) before publishing.

## Do you just design, or build it too?
For **marketing websites**, I take it all the way — I design in Figma and build and launch your site in Framer, so you get a fast, responsive site you own. For **products and apps**, I'm a designer, not a developer: I deliver UX/UI, interactive prototypes, and dev-ready handoff, and I work closely with your engineers (or help you find them) to get it built. I'm always upfront about what's design vs. build for your project — no surprises.

## What's it like to work with you? / What's the process?
Strategy first. We start with your goals, your users, and the real constraints — then I map the flows, design, and refine with you at clear milestones. You're never guessing where things stand. I bring order to complexity; you stay involved without being overwhelmed.

## How much does it cost?
**Marketing websites** come as a fixed package, with custom add-ons if your scope goes beyond it — so you know the number up front. **Product design** is bespoke: because every product differs in size and complexity, I scope and quote it after a short discovery call. Either way, you'll know exactly what you're getting before we start — no open-ended meters. See [[services-offer]].

## How long will it take?
Depends on scope, but I keep timelines tight and realistic. A brand website is typically a few weeks; a product sprint runs ~2–3 weeks; ongoing engagements are monthly. I'll give you a real timeline in the proposal, not a vague "it depends."

## What exactly do I get? (deliverables)
Clear, usable deliverables: strategy and flows, polished designs, a prototype where it helps, design-system foundations for products, and a launched site or dev-ready files. No mystery handoffs.

## Will this actually help my business, or just look nice?
Function and outcomes come first — aesthetics close behind. I balance what's valuable to users, what moves your business, and what's practical to build. I've designed work that didn't just look good but *did a job* — e.g., an internal Amazon Alexa prototype that helped sell a product into existence. See [[proof-points]].

## My product/idea is complicated. Can you handle that?
That's the part I love most. I've worked in advanced, fast-moving tech and I'm good at holding many user flows in my head, then cutting through to what users actually care about. Simplifying complex systems is my core strength. (See NBA Top Shot, Doodles in [[proof-points]].)

## You're one person — are you reliable? Will you finish?
Yes. I take complete ownership — at Doodles I came into a complex build partway through and took full control of the design system for an experience that served 70,000+ users. You get a senior partner who's accountable for the outcome, not a vendor who disappears. Clear milestones keep us both on track.

## Where are you based / how do we communicate?
I'm based in the Netherlands and work with clients across timezones. We'll agree on communication rhythm and overlap hours up front, with regular check-ins and clear async updates so you're never waiting in the dark.

## Can you work with my existing team, brand, and stakeholders?
Absolutely — balancing many stakeholders is part of the job. I've collaborated with product teams and leadership and can plug into your brand, your engineers, and your decision-makers without friction.

## Why hire you instead of an agency or a cheaper marketplace?
You get senior strategy *and* senior craft from one accountable person — no agency overhead, no account-manager telephone game, no junior doing the real work. And unlike a cheap marketplace hire, I bring product thinking, not just pixels.

## Who owns the work? What about revisions?
You own the final work. Revisions are built into each package at clear checkpoints so we refine together without scope spiraling.
