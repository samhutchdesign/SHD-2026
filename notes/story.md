# Origin Story & Brand Voice

> Source material for the About page, the brand voice across the site, and the playful-but-professional tone of the quiz. This is the human spine of the whole project.

## The short version (elevator)

I'm Sam Hutch — a product designer who's known this was the work since I was ten. I grew up next to a game developer (my dad), so I learned early that the best products are systems you *play with*, not just look at. I lead with function, sweat the aesthetics, and I'm happiest taking something tangled and advanced and making it feel obvious.

## The longer narrative (raw material — edit for the page)

**Knew at 10.** Most people fall into design. I chose it as a kid — I discovered it was a real job you could have and never looked back. That's a long time pointed in one direction, and it shows up as conviction: I'm not figuring out whether I want to do this.

**Raised on games.** My father is a game developer. I grew up playing, watching things get built, and absorbing how interaction, feedback, and reward actually work. Games are the most demanding interface design there is — if it isn't intuitive and rewarding, people just stop playing. I bring that sense of **play and whimsy** into product work wherever it fits, and a deep instinct for flow and feedback.

**Function first, aesthetics close behind.** I believe a product has to *work* before it's allowed to be pretty — but pretty still matters. Aesthetics build trust, communicate care, and make the right action obvious. I won't ship something that looks great and confuses people, and I won't ship something that works but feels cheap.

**I balance three forces:** user needs, business needs, and practical scoping. A lot of designers optimize one and ignore the other two. The job is the trade-off — what's genuinely valuable to the user, what moves the business, and what we can actually build in the time and budget we have.

**I simplify complex systems.** This is the core strength. I can hold many user flows in my head at once and map them out — the whole maze, not just one path. I've worked in advanced, fast-moving tech, and the thing I love most is cutting through the complexity to find what the user *actually* cares about, then designing around that.

## Voice & tone guidelines

- **Confident, not corporate.** Plain, direct sentences. First person.
- **Playful where it earns it.** A little whimsy in microcopy and motion — never at the expense of clarity.
- **Show the thinking.** Lead with strategy and reasoning, not just polished screens. The work is a sales asset, not a gallery.
- **Warm and human.** Testimonials describe a "true design partner" — thoughtful, empathetic, collaborative. Keep that.
