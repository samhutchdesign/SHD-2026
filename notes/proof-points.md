# Proof Points — Case Studies & Skills Mapped to Client Pains

> Each piece of work exists on the site to answer a specific client worry. A portfolio is a sales asset, not a gallery — every case study should make a point.

## Featured case studies

### NBA Top Shot
- **What it was:** A digital collecting platform bringing the excitement of real-world collecting to NBA fans online (emerging tech / Web3 consumer product).
- **Pain it answers:** "Can you design a consumer product in a brand-new, complex space and make it feel approachable?" / "Can you handle scale and ambiguity?"
- **Pillars it proves:** Simplify complexity (#2), senior craft (#3), emerging-tech fluency.
- **Track:** B (product), but a recognizable name that builds trust for everyone.
- **Story angle:** Taking an unfamiliar, technical concept (digital collectibles) and making it feel as intuitive and joyful as the real-world thing.

### Doodles
- **What it was:** Managed a complex design system for "CAMP × Doodles in Chicago," a major brand experience initiative; came in partway through a complex build, took complete control, served 70,000+ users.
- **Pain it answers:** "Can you bring order to a complex, in-flight project?" / "Can you own a design system and a stakeholder-heavy initiative?"
- **Pillars it proves:** Senior craft & design systems (#3), simplify complexity (#2), reliability under pressure.
- **Track:** B (product), and a strong reliability/ownership proof point for any client.
- **Story angle:** Parachuting into chaos midstream and taking complete control — directly answers the solo-freelancer reliability fear (see [[faq-objections]]).

### Amazon Alexa *(coming soon — placeholder until assets provided)*

- **Pillars it proves:** Strategy built in (#1), simplify the advanced (#2), business outcomes over visuals.
