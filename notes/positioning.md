# Positioning

> The market stance for Sam Hutch Design. Everything on the site — copy, quiz results, offers — ladders up to this.

## One-line positioning statement (working drafts)

**Primary:**
> Product design & strategy for teams building something new — I turn complex, ambitious ideas into products people understand on the first try.

**Alternates to test:**
- "I'm a product designer who simplifies complex systems — and makes them feel like play."
- "Strategy-led product design for startups and brands who refuse to ship confusing things."
- "From tangled idea to obvious product. Senior product design, strategy, and UX — one partner, start to finish."

## The strategic call (decided)

**Lead premium, accept web work.** Sam's most ownable, differentiated value is *senior product design + strategy + simplifying advanced tech* (Doodles, NBA Top Shot, Amazon Alexa). That's the headline. Visitors who "just need a website" are still welcomed and routed to a simpler, productized offer — but the site does **not** lead with "I make websites," because that's commoditized and undersells the strategic work.

## Value proposition

For founders and teams building something new or complex, Sam Hutch Design delivers **the strategic thinking of a product lead and the craft of a senior designer — in one person, without the agency overhead.** You get someone who can hold the whole system in their head, find what users actually care about, and ship something that works *and* delights.

## "Why me" pillars (the four reasons to hire Sam)

1. **Strategy is built in, not bolted on.** I start with goals, users, and trade-offs — not pixels. You leave with a direction, not just deliverables. *(Backed by: product strategy strength, "balance user + business + scope.")*
2. **I simplify complexity.** Advanced tech, tangled flows, many stakeholders — I cut to the core of what users care about and make it obvious. *(Backed by: NBA Top Shot, Alexa, "hold many flows in my head.")*
3. **Senior craft, end to end.** From strategy and UX through polished UI and design systems — one accountable partner, no handoffs lost in translation. *(Backed by: Doodles design system, 70k+ users; Parsons; mid–senior experience.)*
4. **Function first, with a sense of play.** Raised on games — I design for flow, feedback, and a little whimsy. It works first; it's a pleasure second. *(Backed by: [[story]].)*

## Whitespace / differentiation

Most senior freelance product designers position as either (a) generic "UX/UI designer for hire" or (b) pure strategist with thin visual craft. Sam's wedge: **strategy + senior visual craft + emerging-tech fluency + a distinctive play/whimsy voice**, sold as a personal, single-partner practice. The game-design lens and "simplify the advanced" story are the memorable hooks competitors don't have. See [[competitors]].
