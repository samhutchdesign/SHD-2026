# Audience / Ideal Client Profiles

> Who the site (and quiz) is built for. Two tracks, both routed through the same quiz. Track A is the near-term volume; Track B is the work Sam wants more of.

## Track A — Small business / brand website

**Who:** Solo founders, small-business owners, creators, early-stage brands who need a website or a refreshed online presence.

**What they say they want:** "I need a website."

**What they actually want:** To look credible and trustworthy, to stop losing customers to a confusing or dated site, to feel proud to send people their link. They're buying *confidence and legitimacy*, not pages.

**Pains / triggers:**
- Current site is DIY, dated, or non-existent; embarrassed to share it.
- Launching something and need to look real on day one.
- Getting traffic but not inquiries/sales — the site doesn't convert.
- Overwhelmed by DIY builders and don't want to become a web designer.

**What wins them:** Clear scope, a fixed starting price, a fast and low-stress process, proof it'll look great on mobile. Reassurance they won't be left hanging.

**Risk:** Price-sensitive, can be commoditized. Keep this productized and efficient so it's profitable without dragging Sam into a race to the bottom.

## Track B — Startup / scaleup product & strategy (the goal client)

**Who:** Founders and product teams at funded startups / scaleups building an app, platform, or complex product — often in advanced or emerging tech.

**What they say they want:** "Help us design our product" / "our UX is a mess" / "we need to figure out what to build."

**What they actually want:** A senior partner who can bring order to complexity, align stakeholders, and make the product obvious to users — without the cost and friction of an agency or a full-time hire. They're buying *clarity, momentum, and reduced risk*.

**Pains / triggers:**
- Product has grown complex; users are confused; flows are tangled.
- Pre-build: need strategy and direction before committing engineering.
- Raised money and need to level up the design before the next milestone.
- Have engineers but no senior design/strategy leadership.
- Stakeholders pulling in different directions; need someone to balance and decide.

**What wins them:** Evidence of strategic thinking, experience simplifying advanced tech, design-systems maturity, ability to work with their team and stakeholders, senior judgment. Case studies that show *thinking*, not just screens.

**Why Sam fits:** Directly maps to his strengths — product strategy, simplifying complex systems, holding many flows in his head, emerging-tech experience. See [[positioning]] and [[proof-points]].

## How the quiz serves both

The quiz's first question ("What are you building?") sorts visitors toward a track within one question, then tailors urgency/context and ends on the right offer + book-a-call. Track A → productized website offer. Track B → product/strategy engagement. "Not sure / strategy" → a strategy entry point. See [[quiz-spec]] (Phase 2) and [[services-offer]].
